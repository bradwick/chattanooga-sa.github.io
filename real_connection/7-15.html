July 15

Back On Track

As we go through the day we pause, when agitated or doubtful, and ask for the right thought or action (AA 87).


My agitation begins as we take the “wrong” car to work this morning. I think we should have taken the other one. In my mind I start compiling a list of every reason why my wife’s decision to take this car was wrong. My mouth stays shut, but as I pay more attention to her wrongs, I pay less attention to driving safely.


Fortunately, my years in SA recovery kick in, and I quietly turn to my Higher Power. I hear the question: Is being upset about taking this car to work worth losing your serenity, which could lead you to act out? Of course not! I ask my Higher Power what is the next right action. The answers I get are: Surrender your resentment. Focus on the present moment and concentrate on driving safely. Oh, and also be grateful you have more than one car.


No longer is my mind filled with negative thoughts. With each mile of purposeful driving my mind clears and my serenity blossoms. I am back on track. When I drop off my wife, I am smiling and laughing—all due to working the Steps on my problem.


Higher Power, thank you for granting me the courage to live the SA principles each day.
