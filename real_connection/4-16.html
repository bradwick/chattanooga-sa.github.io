April 16

The Root Of The Problem

Admitted to God, to ourselves, and to another human being the exact nature of our wrongs (AA 59).


After working several inventories and sharing them, I realized the obvious eight hundred pound gorilla in the working of the Fifth Step. Nature (singular), wrongs (plural); there was one basic problem, one source of all my character defects and harmful actions: self-centered fear. All my disturbances traced to this root. The only one my decision-making process took into consideration was myself.


I denied that we are all in this together, that life is a collective effort, with no person being of greater importance than someone else. When one ignores a truth so vast, it can only result in a fierce internal struggle. It was impossible to reconcile with myself the delusion that I should take preference above all others. The only thing this produced was terror, which gave birth to my other defects—my failed attempts to deal with my fears.


The solution was to uncover this fundamental truth and to apply it to my decision making. During my healing, many of my shortcomings were easily dealt with. I realized that self-centered fear was “the exact nature” of all my wrong views and actions. Recovery was simplified: search out, expose, and let go of anything that keeps me sick, and then help others do the same.


God, I have been consumed by self-centeredness. I now expose this wound, abandon fear, embrace love, and accept my proper place in humanity.
